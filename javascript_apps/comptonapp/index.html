Gamma-rays that undergo Compton scattering in a radiation detector without depositing their remaining energy through Photoelectric absorption in the same detector are undesirable in gamma-ray spectroscopy. That is because without full energy deposition it is not possible to identify which isotope produced the gamma-ray. Experimentally, a continuum of energies is observed corresponding to gamma-rays scattering across the angular range up to 180 degrees. The “Compton Edge” is the maximum energy that can be deposited by a gamma-ray Compton scattering.
